What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the protection of computer systems, networks, and digital information from cyber attacks, unauthorized access, and data breaches. It involves technologies, processes, and practices designed to defend against malicious activity.

The Importance of Cybersecurity

In today’s digital world, nearly every activity depends on technology — online banking, education, communication, shopping, and healthcare. Without cybersecurity, sensitive information such as passwords, personal data, financial records, and government data could be stolen or manipulated.

The CIA Triad

Cybersecurity is built around three core principles known as the CIA Triad:

  • Confidentiality – Ensuring information is only accessible to authorized users.
  • Integrity – Preventing unauthorized changes to data.
  • Availability – Making sure systems and data are accessible when needed.

Types of Cybersecurity

  • Network Security – Protecting internal networks from intrusions.
  • Application Security – Securing software and applications.
  • Information Security – Protecting sensitive data.
  • Cloud Security – Securing cloud-based systems and storage.
  • Operational Security – Policies and procedures to protect data handling.

Real-World Impact

Cyber attacks can cause financial losses, reputational damage, and service disruption. Large companies, hospitals, and even governments have experienced cyber attacks that resulted in millions of euros in damage.
