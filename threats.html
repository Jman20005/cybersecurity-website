1. Phishing

Phishing is a social engineering attack where criminals trick users into revealing personal information such as passwords or credit card details.

Example: Fake emails pretending to be from banks or delivery companies asking you to click a malicious link.

2. Malware

Malware stands for malicious software. It is designed to damage systems, steal data, or spy on users.

Types of Malware:

  • Viruses
  • Worms
  • Trojans
  • Spyware
  • Adware

3. Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts files or locks systems and demands payment to restore access. It has affected hospitals, businesses, and public services worldwide.

4. Social Engineering

Manipulating individuals into giving away confidential information. This attack targets human psychology rather than technical systems.

5. DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) Attacks

A DDoS attack overwhelms a server or website with massive traffic, causing it to crash and become unavailable to legitimate users.

6. Insider Threats

Insider threats occur when employees or authorized users misuse their access intentionally or accidentally, leading to data breaches.

7. Zero-Day Exploits

These attacks exploit vulnerabilities in software before developers have had the chance to fix them.

Why Understanding Threats is Important

Understanding different cyber threats allows individuals and organizations to take preventive measures and reduce the risk of becoming victims.

Knowing the Threats is the First Step to Prevention.
